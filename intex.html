Introduction

The American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) is an American professional association that, in its own words, "promotes the art, science, and practice of multidisciplinary engineering and allied sciences around the globe" via "continuing education, training and professional development, codes and standards, research, conferences and publications, government relations, and other forms of outreach."

ASME TKMCE was established on November 2018 under the guidance and leadership of Dr. Rijo Jacob Thomas, Assistant Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, Mr. Goutham B Raj (Mechanical Batch 2016-2020 batch) and Mr. Adilsha Kizhuvedath (both students of Mechanical Batch 2016-2020 batch). The Establishment Certificate by ASME is given.

1. E FEST VIT

ASME runs several annual E-Fests, or Engineering Festivals, taking the place of the Student Professional Development Conference (SPDC) series. In addition to the Human Powered Vehicle Challenge (HPVC), the Innovative Additive Manufacturing 3D Challenge (IAM3D), the Student Design Competition, and the Old Guard Competition, there are also talks, interactive workshops, and entertainment. These events allow students to network with working engineers, host contests, and promote ASME's benefits to students as well as professionals.

ASMEs E-Fest Asia Pacific 2019 was held from 1st – 3rd February 2019 at Vellore Institute of Technology, Vellore. Students of ASME TKMCE participated in various competitions including Student Design Competition (SDC), Innovative Additive Manufacturing 3D Challenge (IAM3D), Aeromania (RC plane competition) and other talks, interactive workshops.

SDC CHALLENGE

Team OPTIMUS which comprised of 19 students under the leadership of Niranjana MT and Sayuj Jayadev, took part in the Student Design Competition (SDC), which involved to create a device that can quickly but carefully secure a variety of different balls that will be balancing on tube stands in the middle of a flat playing surface. You must construct a single remotely controlled device to collect as many balls as possible, and place them in a collection area – you must do this quickly, but avoid having balls fall off their stand and hit the ground.The team was able to qualify the technical rounds but failed in the knockout round.

IAM 3D CHALLENGE

Team SEDNA comprising of 15 students under the leadership of Nithin Prabhakaran and Prennoy Babu participated in the Innovative Additive Manufacturing 3D Challenge (IAM3D), which to design and manufacture a hovercraft using additive manufacturing that is capable of picking up and carrying a payload through a ground course and deliver the payload to its final destination. The team qualified all the rounds but couldn’t complete the final round due to technical difficulty and got a verbal mention for design.

AEROMANIA

Four teams of 5 students participated in Aeromania Challenge. Team NANDINI under Vipin R, Team FALCON under Sarath Mohan, Team HAWK EYE under Siddarth R Nair, Team XR under Godwin Andrew. The challenge requires students to design and fabricate a RC Plane capable of performing a set of manoeuvres and completing the course. Out of which team NANDINI secured the 3rd prize.

3. UNIVERSITY MARS ROVER CHALLENGE

The Mars Society’s University Rover Challenge challenges students to build remotely operated rovers that can accomplish a variety of tasks that might one-day assist astronauts working on the surface of Mars

Rovers will compete in four missions:

  1. A science mission to investigate a site for the presence of current or past life
  2. A delivery mission to deliver a variety of objects to astronauts in the field across rugged terrain
  3. An equipment servicing mission to perform dexterous operations on a mock lander using a robotic arm
  4. An autonomous navigation mission to autonomously travel to a site and locate a marker
The team for URC is formed in collaboration with IEDC TKMCE and the Indian Rover Challenge will be held in VIT 2020.

4. E FEST MARVADI

ASMEs E-Fest Asia Pacific 2020 was held from 28th February – 1st March 2020 at Marwadi University, Rajkot. Students of ASME TKMCE participated in various competitions including Human Powered Vehicle Challenge (HPVC), Innovative Additive Manufacturing 3D Challenge (IAM3D), Aeromania (RC plane competition), Biomimicry Challenge and other talks, interactive workshops.

IAM 3D CHALLENGE

Team AETHER comprising of 13 students under the leadership of Nithin Prabhakaran and Prennoy Babu participated in the Innovative Additive Manufacturing 3D Challenge (IAM3D), which to design and manufacture a Unmanned Aerial Racing Cargo Vehicle (U.A.R.C.V) using additive manufacturing that is capable of picking up and carrying a payload through a course and deliver the payload to its final destination. The team was very successful in completing all the round and was the only team who manufactured the drone completely using additive manufacturing. The team secured 9th position in the Asia Pacific zone.

Human Powered Vehicle Challenge

Team NATUS comprising of 27 students under the leadership of Avinash E Babu and Nikhil S Participated in HPVC challenge which was to demonstrate the application of sound engineering design principles in the development of sustainable and practical transportation alternatives. In the HPVC, students work in teams to design and build efficient, highly engineered vehicles for everyday use. The team won 7th position in Endurance racing and 10th position in Female racing. The team secured 23rd position in the Asia Pacific zone.

AEROMANIA

Two teams of 5 students participated in Aeromania Challenge. Team JADAYU under Hardik Ramath, Team DHARMA under M S Balaji. The challenge requires students to design and fabricate a RC Plane capable of performing a set of manoeuvres and completing the course. All the teams were able to clear the technical round.

Biomimicry Challenge

The Challenge was to find a Solutions for global challenge. Innovate, create and design more functional need-based solutions within the limited availability of natural resources. A team comprising of Athul TP, Prennoy babu, Abin S, Arun Krishna, Hardik Ramath won the special appreciation award.
